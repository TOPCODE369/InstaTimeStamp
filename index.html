How it works

Three steps to the exact timestamp.

Instagram encodes the post time inside every post URL. We decode it — no API, no scraping, no account needed.

1

Find the post on Instagram

Open Instagram on your phone or browser and find the post or reel you want to check.

2

Copy the link

Tap the three-dot menu → Copy link. On desktop, copy the URL from your browser's address bar.

3

Paste and check

Paste the link above and hit Check time. The exact date, time, and post age appear instantly.

4

Why it works

Every Instagram post ID contains an encoded Unix timestamp. InstaTimestamp decodes it locally in your browser — no servers involved.

FAQ

Common questions.

Does this work for private Instagram accounts?
+
No. InstaTimestamp works only with public posts and reels. Private account URLs will not return a valid result since they require authentication to access.
Does it work for Instagram Stories?
+
Not currently. Stories use a different URL format and expire after 24 hours. InstaTimestamp supports posts (/p/) and reels (/reel/) only.
Is the timestamp 100% accurate?
+
It is accurate to within a second. The timestamp is decoded directly from Instagram's post ID — the same data Instagram uses internally. It is not an estimate.
Do you store the URLs I paste?
+
No. All decoding happens inside your browser using JavaScript. Nothing you paste is ever sent to our servers. We have no access to the URLs you check.
Why does Instagram hide exact post times?
+
Instagram shows relative times like "3 days ago" to keep the interface clean — especially on mobile. The exact time is available on desktop by hovering over the timestamp, but this is completely hidden on the mobile app.
